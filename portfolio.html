About

Lylian Thao, a Hmong-American artist from the Bay Area, uses her art to address meaningful topics and inspire change. Drawing from her family and cultural background, her work focuses on the artistic responsibilities of Hmong women and aims to educate and empower them.

Her art incorporates symbolism, encourages self-reflection, and promotes themes of self-love and self-care. Through her creations, she honors her heritage, inspires reflection, and advocates for positive change, emphasizing the value and importance of every individual's life.

Artist Statement

My art draws upon my Hmong-American heritage to explore themes of identity, self-love, and cultural empowerment. Through digital illustrations and 3D models, I create immersive narratives that inspire empathy and introspection.

I use symbolism to delve into personal and collective stories, inviting viewers to reflect on their own experiences. My goal is to foster understanding and positive change, emphasizing the importance of honoring one's heritage and embracing self-care.
